Content Production for a Fintech Product

Context

Cryptonix is an infrastructure company that processes crypto payments. When I joined the project, the company already had a blog, but the process for creating new articles could not support the number of publications planned as part of the strategy.

Challenge

The challenge was to reach a pace of 4 publications per month and improve their quality in terms of SEO optimization and user retention.

The goals were to achieve the following:

  • Increase the number of articles published per month from 1 to 4.

  • Increase CTR by at least 1.5 times.

  • Double the average session duration.

My Role

My role was to ensure that the existing team could achieve the goals described above.

Available resources:

  • Part-time SEO specialist.

  • Copywriting agency.

  • Website development team.

  • Communications manager.

Building a Scalable Content Process

The key reason behind the low content efficiency and slow production speed was the fragmented knowledge of the product and its goals. The SEO specialist and the agency were not involved deeply enough in the product to plan and create content that would be relevant to the target audience and reflect what the company could offer them.

The solution was to create a detailed product knowledge base in a structured format that removed ambiguity. However, even with this knowledge base, the speed of the copywriting agency still left much to be desired, and after a short experiment we decided to stop using their services in favor of AI.

At the same time, the SEO specialist could use this knowledge base to conduct more accurate market analysis and create more relevant content hypotheses. The article structure resulting from his work provided enough input to create articles with high uniqueness, low fluff, and low keyword stuffing.

All that was needed before publication was a final check to ensure that the tone of voice of the finished article matched the brand. The communications manager, who previously spent up to 10 hours reviewing agency texts, now spent about 1 hour correcting the result.

The issue of user retention still remained. Before looking for the cause in the content itself, I noticed that the blog had problems with hierarchy, spacing, line length, and other small details that together could make long texts uncomfortable to read. After carrying out a light redesign together with the website development team, we saw an increase in the average time users spent on blog pages.

Results

By removing bottlenecks, the speed of content development increased and the target metrics were achieved.

As a result:

  • The team reached a pace of 4 articles per month.

  • The average user session duration increased by 2.5 times.

  • CTR almost doubled.

As a bonus, the costs of working with the agency were eliminated, the communications manager’s time was saved, and the redesign improved the performance of previously published content as well.
