Creative Production for Performance Marketing

Context

When I joined the team at Incentive Agency, it planned and launched marketing campaigns for an Official Apple Reseller in three regions and was preparing to scale to seven more. This significant growth required optimization and the removal of bottlenecks and risks in the creative production process needed to launch campaigns.

Challenge

The challenge was to build a reliable process for creating creatives that could handle peak workload periods when campaigns were launched across all markets at roughly the same time.

In numbers, this meant that within a period of 2–3 weeks, the team had to produce animated creatives for 50–80 separate campaigns, including their adaptations for different placements.

My Role

My role was to build this process, including:

  • Reducing dependency on freelancers.

  • Building an internal design team.

  • Developing workflows and a methodology for tracking them.

  • Moving quality control out of the client managers’ area of responsibility.

Rebuilding the Production Process

It was impossible to immediately abandon the old process, which depended on one freelance designer who both created layouts and animated the creatives, because he was still responsible for ongoing tasks. During the gradual transition, based on the results of our joint work, I identified the main production stages for which I needed to build a team.

The process included three steps:

  • Design

  • Animation

  • Quality control

Animation changed only slightly from launch to launch and from creative to creative, so it could be templated. Design, on the other hand, although based on the same templates, could change radically depending on regional offers, requests from the client’s representatives in different regions, and even the language used. This determined the structure of the future team:

  • 2 junior graphic designers

  • 1 motion designer

A cross-check quality control process was also implemented within this team and became a standard step when transferring tasks from one production stage to another. The full transition away from the freelancer took one and a half months, during which we organized the accumulated data and materials into a curated design system and successfully tested the new team in real production conditions.

Results

The new process gave us a more scalable and organized production system, which we applied across all markets.

  • We successfully scaled from 3 to 10 regional markets.

  • Operational risks related to dependency on a single contractor decreased.

  • Campaign launches became much easier to coordinate and support.

The new process replaced a fragile model with a scalable system capable of supporting simultaneous launches across multiple markets.
