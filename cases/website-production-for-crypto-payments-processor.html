Web Production for a Fintech Product

Context

Cryptonix is an infrastructure company that processes crypto payments. When I joined the project, the team had an ambitious goal: to launch one hundred new landing pages within a year. These pages were needed for marketing campaigns and search engine optimization. However, the actual launch pace did not match these plans.

Challenge

The challenge was to set up the production of new pages within the available resources and deliver the following within a year:

  • 100 new landing pages for marketing campaigns.

  • Search-optimized pages to support a threefold increase in organic traffic.

  • 4 widgets serving as lead magnets.

My Role

My role was to ensure that the existing team could achieve the goals described above.

Available resources:

  • Part-time SEO specialist.

  • Copywriting agency.

  • UX/UI designer.

  • Part-time frontend developer.

  • Communications manager.

Building a Scalable Production System

The bottleneck slowing down the process was the lack of information available to team members when they received tasks.

The SEO specialist, who was not deeply enough involved in the product, passed technical briefs to the copywriting agency, whose writers understood the product even less. As a result, the texts required multiple rounds of review from the communications manager and the Cryptonix team. While the texts were still in progress, the designer had to prepare layouts based on drafts, which led to additional revisions after the final texts were approved. As a result, new pages reached the frontend developer not gradually, but in large batches, making it harder to focus on quality execution.

The first step in fixing the situation was creating a detailed product knowledge base in a structured format that removed ambiguity. During an experiment, we found that with this knowledge base in place, AI produced better results than the agency in both quality and speed, so we stopped working with the agency. The briefs prepared by the SEO specialist using this knowledge base were enough to generate texts within one day instead of several weeks.

This meant that the designer received not a draft that could still change, but final texts that helped avoid unnecessary revisions and allowed her to account for how real content would behave across different screen sizes. This stage was also optimized by unifying the design system into a set of reusable blocks. Previously, the website inherited several separate design systems from different stages of its history, which led to blocks being reinvented repeatedly. A single source of truth reduced the time required to create each individual page.

By eliminating revisions that could catch up with performers from previous stages and removing bottlenecks across the entire production line, we made sure that tasks reached development more evenly, allowing the developer to distribute the workload more effectively.

It is also worth noting that content and design did not provide enough information to create widgets. As a result, the developer could unintentionally make mistakes in the logic of calculations and cryptocurrency display, which may be non-obvious to someone outside the domain. To prevent such errors, we used AI prototyping: the full logic was already available to the developer at the technical brief stage, so he only had to focus on implementing it within the website.

Results

As a result of these changes, the team reached the target production pace, and the quality of the pages began to match the business goals.

Within one year:

  • All 100 planned pages were launched.

  • 4 lead magnet widgets were launched.

  • Organic traffic grew proportionally to the volume of new pages, increasing by approximately 3.5 times.

Additionally, the workload on the team and the involvement of individual stakeholders, including top management, in approval processes decreased, allowing them to focus on other strategically important tasks.
