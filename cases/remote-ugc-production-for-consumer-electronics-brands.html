UGC Production for a Consumer Electronics Brand

Context

When I joined the team at Incentive Agency, it was managing social media accounts for an Official Apple Reseller in three regions and preparing to scale to seven more. The pages followed the style of Apple’s official account and consisted mostly of #ShotOniPhone content. But as the need to scale emerged, the client also requested video-format UGC content, which required a complete rebuild of the production process.

Challenge

The challenge was to build a reliable process that could support the content plan for 10 regional accounts. Regional specifics made direct content reuse impossible, so the team had to produce 120 pieces of content per month — 3 per week for each account. This included:

  • 1/3 content under the #ShotOniPhone hashtag.

  • 2/3 UGC content.

Additional content also had to be published situationally for promotions and offers.

My Role

My role involved building and coordinating a production process capable of delivering the required content volume.

This included:

  • Finding and onboarding creators.

  • Organizing product logistics for shoots.

  • Coordinating script development, filming, editing, and publication.

  • Optimizing production costs and contractor allocation.

Building a Distributed Production Network

Producing turnkey UGC content using only regional creators would have seemed logical, but it would have required excessive costs and organizational overhead. As the creator’s level increased, the cost often went up while the speed of content production went down. To achieve the required productivity, we needed to build a production pipeline where each stage was predictable and controlled.

First, we organized a centralized script production process: one writer could analyze trends and references and use them to create and adapt scripts for all markets. This approach removed the need to create and approve 80 separate scripts and instead required around 10 scripts prepared for further translation. Adaptation also did not require additional effort from the writer, since it happened naturally through differences in creators’ equipment and production approaches.

The next stage was translation. To avoid limiting the choice of creators by language knowledge, this part of the pipeline was handled by professional translators. For some markets, the client provided in-house translators, while external contractors were brought in for others.

Then the scripts were divided into those that could be produced regardless of location — tutorials or atmospheric videos — and those that could only be produced in a specific region: content with new device models, sketches, and other videos involving actors.

For the first type of scripts, the entire production process was handled internally: we hired an in-house content creator who produced versions of these scripts for all regional markets.

For the second type of content, local creators were found in each market. They had the required equipment, knew the locations, could obtain devices for filming from the client, and could involve people in the shoots.

To minimize dependency on these contractors, editing, subtitles, and video processing were also handled internally by one motion designer.

As a result, the UGC production process did not fully match the “user” part of the name, but it was reliable and predictable.

The resources saved through this approach allowed us to work with stronger creators for #ShotOniPhone content, and this format effectively turned into collaboration with micro-influencers and their audiences. We also proposed and launched content under the #DrawOniPad hashtag, which helped promote the iPad and engage creators and their audiences in the digital art niche.

Results

The process described above enabled predictable and manageable production of a large content volume without delays and within budget.

Over the course of a year, the agency produced:

  • Around 1,000 pieces of UGC content.

  • Around 400 pieces of content under the #ShotOniPhone and #DrawOniPad hashtags.

As a result, we supplied the Official Apple Reseller’s social media accounts with the content required to support growth within their brand promotion strategy.
